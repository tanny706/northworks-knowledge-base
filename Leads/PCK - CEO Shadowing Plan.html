What changes because it's one day
  • "Deal-active" is do-or-die. Day must include ≥1 international fund call + ≥1 credit decision / deal review + ≥1 team interaction with the 5 staff. If those three aren't all on the calendar — pick a different day.
  • Pre-day call: 30 min → 60 min. Identify which weekly patterns we will not see live and need summarized debriefs on later.
  • Backup day is mandatory in case the chosen day collapses.
  • Synthesis cycle compresses — no "between days" pause to integrate Day 1 before Day 2.
  • Risk: curated day. Pick a day he can't control (deal-crisis, end-of-month, post-international-call) rather than a "nice" day.
What changes because we're skipping staff 1:1s
  • Joy's role intensifies. No longer floating observer-coordinator — she is the primary instrument for staff perspective through ambient observation, hallway conversation, lunch-table presence.
  • Capture staff–Khun Nut interaction moments verbatim. Body language, tone, who initiates, who avoids. The team's reactions to him are the proxy for what staff 1:1s would have surfaced.
  • Lunch / break / transition time = data. Joy stays in the room when Khun Nut steps out.
  • Flag the gap explicitly in the written summary — "here is what we couldn't see from observation alone."

2Why We're Doing This

Internal alignment + the pitch to Khun Nut

For Our Team — Sai Fah La Mun Alignment

1He is the system right now
With 5 staff (down from 25–30), the "operating model" is whatever Khun Nut does day-to-day. We can't redesign what we haven't seen.
2Two failed MDs prove secondhand fails
Both MDs presumably "understood" the brief and still produced drama or abuse. We can't repeat that — we need our own primary data.
3"Ownership mindset" needs operational definition
He uses the phrase the way founders use "culture" — meaning a hundred things at once. We have to watch what he reinforces, tolerates, and corrects to define it concretely enough to hire for and train to.
4"Too kind" is a behavior, not a self-description
We need to see the actual moments — when he softens a directive, when he absorbs a mistake himself, when he picks up "ไม้เรียว" but doesn't swing — to design a disciplinary framework he will actually use.
5De-risks the Roadmap presentation
When we present back to him, credibility comes from "we watched this happen on Tuesday at 3pm" — not "best practice says."

3What We Want to Learn

Mapped to downstream deliverables · every observation has a destination

Observe his language
2A
→ Vision / Mission / Positioning
  • Phrases he repeats unprompted about PCK, clients, team
  • How he describes PCK to outsiders vs. internally — the gap reveals positioning truth
  • What makes him animated vs. flat — genuine vision shows up in energy
  • Stories he chooses to retell — Forestias, Beckham, fund wins — the company's de facto mythology
Tool: verbatim quote log → mine for Vision/Mission draft
Observe the work, not the titles
2B
→ Org Chart + headcount plan
  • Who actually does what among the 5 (2 legal + 3 credit)
  • Work he is doing that should not be a CEO's work — these are the missing roles
  • Work falling through cracks — these are the blind-spot roles
  • What the 6 subsidiaries pull from him — parent headcount must absorb subsidiary load
Tool: work-log grid (5 staff × time blocks × activity)
Observe what he reinforces
2C
→ "Ownership Mindset" codification · MOST IMPORTANT
  • Moments of praise — what behavior triggered it? That's "ownership"
  • Moments of frustration — what triggered it? That's the "employee mindset" to eliminate
  • Ask: which past or current staff had the mindset he wants? Real examples beat abstract values
  • The Thai word he reaches for: เจ้าของ? นักรบ? ลูกหม้อ? Vocabulary = culture
Tool: Reinforces / Frustrates two-column log — target 15–20 entries each side
Observe him training
2D
→ Onboarding & training system
  • Actual content — product, deal structuring, client handling
  • Method — Socratic, lecture, show-and-do, war stories
  • Sequence — what comes first, second, third
  • Implicit prerequisites he assumes the trainee knows
  • Where he gets impatient or repeats — recurring training gaps
Tool: ask him to monologue-train a hypothetical new RM — record with consent
Observe the deal cycle
2E
→ Milestone-based commission structure
  • Actual stages: sourcing → docs → negotiation → legal → board → close. Timestamps matter
  • Which stages take longest = cash-flow pain points for RMs
  • Who touches the deal at each stage
  • Where deals die — and where RM "ownership" actually pays off
Tool: deal-cycle flowchart sketched live as he walks us through real deals
Observe his judgment calls
2F
→ SOPs with risk-grading (1-2-3-4)
  • How he risk-grades a loan in real time
  • Intuitive heuristics not written down — these are SOP gold
  • Questions he asks his credit team = SOP checklist items
  • The line between 1-2 (junior-decidable) and 3-4 (CEO-decidable)
Quiet-moment ask: "ถ้าจะให้น้องเครดิตตัดสินใจเองได้ในเคสเกรด 1-2 คุณณัฐต้องเห็นอะไรในเอกสารบ้างถึงจะวางใจ?"
Shadow Khun Chon directly
2G
→ EA JD (for Khun Chon) + RM JD
  • What Khun Chon actually does day-to-day — JD must match this, not a generic template
  • Where Khun Chon is bottlenecked = the EA's job
  • Interaction pattern Khun Nut ↔ Khun Chon — informs EA's authority boundaries
  • What kind of person could thrive between these two execs = cultural fit screen
Tool: separate 2-hour block with Khun Chon, same week (locked decision)
Observe decision points
2H
→ Decentralized management + NDA framework
  • Every decision that lands on his desk — log + categorize (SOP-able / manager-able / true CEO)
  • Information he sees that others don't = confidentiality boundaries
  • What he refuses to delegate — distinguish won't vs. can't vs. shouldn't
  • Sensitive material flowing through informal channels (Line, verbal) needing NDA
Tool: decision log — every CEO desk-landing categorized in real time
Observe the moments
2I
→ Disciplinary framework · MOST SENSITIVE
  • A mistake / underperformance moment — does he soften, absorb, avoid, or use "ไม้เรียว" symbolically?
  • Aftermath — does behavior recur? Does the person learn?
  • His narration ("I should have said something but...") — surface gently in debrief
  • What he does do well disciplinarily — build the framework around that, not against him
Design intent: structured "private feedback + internal written record + escalation path that doesn't require him to wield the cane himself" — likely future MD / HR lead carries the harder conversations
Observe his energy curve
2J
→ Founder bandwidth + sustainability
  • When his energy / judgment are sharpest (likely morning) — protect for strategic work
  • When fatigue shows (likely late afternoon) — hours to systematize, not lean on him
  • Which late-night blocks are genuinely irreplaceable (intl time zones) vs. habit
  • His "off" moments — gym, commute, meals — windows for short syncs
End-of-day capture: "อะไรที่วันนี้พลังงานหมดไปกับเรื่องที่ไม่น่าจะต้องทำเองครับ/คะ?"

What NOT to Do · Hard Lines

Discipline this. The day fails if any of these slip.

  • Don't interrogate. Shadowing, not interview. Questions are for transition moments only.
  • Don't take notes visibly in front of the 5 staff. They will perform or freeze. Step away or capture from memory.
  • Don't speak to staff about Khun Nut at all. Joy is observing ambient, not interviewing. No formal 1:1s.
  • Don't react visibly to "too kind" moments. No raised eyebrows, no "you should have...". Note, debrief privately.
  • Don't reference the failed MDs unprompted. If he raises them, listen. If not, save for the calm 1:1.
  • Don't try to add value during the day. Resist suggesting, advising, fixing. Pure observation IS the value.
  • Don't shadow in the gym, bathroom, family calls, or personal medical/financial calls. Hard line.
  • Don't bring multiple Sai Fah La Mun people into the same meeting. One observer per room max.

Written Summary → Debrief Sequence

5-Section Structure

1. What we saw that's working — lead with strength, earn the right to the hard part.
2. What we saw that surprised us — neutral curiosity, not judgment.
3. The two or three patterns we think are limiting the scale — named directly, with concrete examples from the day, not abstract advice.
4. What we couldn't see in one day — named gaps, so he knows the limits of what we're claiming.
5. What we want to dig deeper on before the Roadmap — preview of the discussion.

Send via private channel · mark "ส่วนตัว — สำหรับคุณณัฐเท่านั้น" · do not share with secretary or staff.

Open the debrief with: "ได้อ่านที่เราส่งไปแล้วใช่ไหมคะ ส่วนไหนที่อยากเริ่มคุยก่อน?" — let him lead. He may push back, agree, expand, or surface things we missed. All of those reactions are gold for the Roadmap design.

7Risks & Mitigations

What can go wrong on the day · and how we handle it before it does

He performs a curated day for us
Elevated · 1 day
Mitigation: Elevated since we're doing one day, not two. Pick a day he can't curate — deal-crisis day, end-of-month, post-international-call with follow-ups landing. Avoid days he or his secretary "set up nicely" for us. The 60-min pre-day call must test for this: if the day looks too tidy, swap to the backup.
The 5 staff freeze up or perform for us
Standard
Mitigation: Joy is ambient — present, casual, not visibly capturing. No clipboard, no questions about Khun Nut. We are not doing formal 1:1s. Brief the staff (via Khun Nut) that Joy is "designing the new system, not evaluating you."
Over-reliance on Khun Nut's framing of his own team
New · No 1:1s
Mitigation: Joy's ambient observation must capture staff-initiated moments — what they talk about when he leaves the room, who they go to with questions, who they avoid. Cross-check Joy's notes against Khun Nut's narrative in the 48-hour synthesis. If the gap is significant, flag in the written summary and propose a follow-up staff conversation post-Roadmap.
We see "too kind" moments and react visibly
Standard
Mitigation: Pre-agreed internal signal between Tanny and Joy — a glance, not a comment. Process in the car, not in the room.
He asks us in the moment "what should I do?"
Standard
Pre-prepared answer: "ขอจดไว้ก่อนนะคะ เดี๋ยว debrief เราจะตอบให้ครบ — ตอนนี้อยากเห็นวิธีคุณณัฐทำก่อน" Resist the urge to advise mid-day; it contaminates the observation.
He shares something deeply personal or confidential
Standard
Mitigation: Listen. Don't take notes on it. Don't include in any written artifact. Treat as a lawyer or priest would. The relational deposit from holding this well is enormous.
The two-failed-MDs topic comes up emotionally
Standard
Mitigation: Let him talk. Don't problem-solve. Don't compare or analyze the two MDs in front of him. Note for ourselves: the wounds will inform the disciplinary framework design.
We confuse "shadowing the CEO" with "auditing the company"
Standard
Mitigation: Scope is HR foundation for the parent company. Not assessing credit book, subsidiaries, M&A, or property portfolio. Stay in lane. If we see something material outside scope (e.g., obvious compliance risk), flag privately in debrief; don't act.
He gets tired mid-afternoon, shadowing becomes a burden
Standard
Mitigation: Build in a soft exit ("we'll step out after the 3pm meeting and reconnect tomorrow morning") so he has breathing room. We can always extend if he wants us to stay.
Cultural — seen as the ฝรั่ง / consultant judging the Thai SME founder
Standard
Mitigation: Thai politeness register throughout — ครับ/ค่ะ, who you ไหว้ first, removing shoes, greeting senior vs. junior staff. Joy can lead on these protocols. Dress for his office, not for Bangkok corporate.
He uses the day to "vent" rather than work normally
Standard
Mitigation: A gift if it happens — capture it. Re-anchor gently: "อยากเห็นคุณณัฐทำงานปกติ ๆ ด้วยนะคะ ระหว่างวัน — เผื่อจะได้เห็นจังหวะจริง ๆ"

8What "Success" Looks Like

If we have all 7 by the end of synthesis, the Roadmap will land · if we don't, we go back

Definition of Done

  1. Describe a normal Tuesday at PCK in 3 paragraphs.
  2. Name the 5 staff and what each actually does (vs. their title) — noting any gaps from skipping 1:1s.
  3. Define "ownership mindset" in 3 observable behaviors, in Thai, in his own words.
  4. Draw the deal cycle from sourcing to close with milestones and rough durations.
  5. Identify the 3 biggest CEO-load drains that could be removed without an MD hire.
  6. Have a designed-for-Khun-Nut disciplinary process that he'll actually use.
  7. Walk into the HR Roadmap presentation with at least 20 verbatim moments that prove we understand his business.
If we have all 7, the Roadmap will land. If we don't, we go back.
