# PCK — CEO Shadowing Plan (Khun Nut)

> A working playbook, not a strategy memo. Every observation block is mapped to a downstream HR deliverable so we know why we're capturing it. Update inline as the day approaches.

---

## 1. Why we're doing this

### 1a. For our internal team (Sai Fah La Mun alignment)

We have committed to delivering an end-to-end HR rebuild — Vision/Mission/Positioning/Org Chart/headcount, onboarding & training, milestone-based commission structure, SOPs with risk-grading, JDs for EA + RM, decentralized management + NDA framework, and codified "ownership mindset" culture. **Every one of those deliverables is grounded in how Khun Nut actually runs the business today.** If we design them off a generic SME template, we will produce the same artifacts the previous two MDs produced, which is exactly the pattern we're being hired to break.

We need direct, unfiltered observation because:

1. **He is the system right now.** With 5 staff (down from 25–30), the "operating model" is whatever Khun Nut does day-to-day. We can't redesign what we haven't seen.
2. **The two failed MDs prove that secondhand interpretation fails.** Both MDs presumably "understood" the brief and still produced drama or abuse. We can't repeat that — we need our own primary data.
3. **"Ownership mindset" needs operational definition.** He uses the phrase the way founders use "culture" — meaning a hundred things at once. We have to watch what he reinforces, tolerates, and corrects to define it concretely enough to hire for and train to.
4. **"Too kind" is a behavior, not a self-description.** We need to see the actual moments — when he softens a directive, when he absorbs a mistake himself, when he picks up "ไม้เรียว" but doesn't swing — to design a disciplinary framework he will actually use.
5. **De-risks the Roadmap presentation.** When we present back to him, the credibility comes from "we watched this happen on Tuesday at 3pm" — not "best practice says."

### 1b. The pitch to Khun Nut (so he gives us the time willingly)

Frame this as **the highest-leverage block of time he can give us before we present the Roadmap.** Suggested talking points (Thai-ready):

> "คุณณัฐครับ/ค่ะ — ก่อนเราจะกลับมาเสนอ HR Roadmap ขอเวลา 1–2 วันเดินตามคุณณัฐแบบ shadowing นะคะ เหตุผลคือ ระบบ HR ที่เราจะออกแบบให้ ถ้ามันไม่ match กับวิธีที่คุณณัฐทำงานจริง มันจะเป็นกระดาษอีกชุดนึงที่ไม่มีใครใช้ — เหมือนที่ MD สองคนก่อนเคยเจอ
>
> สิ่งที่เราจะทำคือ **ดู ไม่ตัดสิน** — ดูจังหวะการทำงาน การคุยกับทีม 5 คน การ train คน การตัดสินใจดีล สิ่งที่คุณณัฐบอกเองว่า 'ไม่รู้ว่าตัวเองไม่รู้อะไร' — เราจะช่วยเป็นกระจกให้
>
> Output ที่คุณณัฐจะได้กลับ: HR Roadmap ที่ออกแบบรอบ ๆ ตัวคุณณัฐและธุรกิจจริง ไม่ใช่ template สำเร็จรูป — และ debrief ส่วนตัวที่บอกตรง ๆ ว่าเราเห็นอะไรที่คุณณัฐอาจจะไม่เห็น"

Three things to make explicit so he commits:
- **It is observation, not evaluation.** We are not grading him; we are documenting the operating reality.
- **He doesn't need to perform or prepare.** Worst outcome is him "showing" us a curated day. We want the real one — chaos included.
- **There is a deliverable he gets personally** — a private debrief, not a public report. This is the carrot for the time investment.

## 2. What we want to learn — mapped to downstream deliverables

Organized **by deliverable**, not by chronology. Each observation block specifies what we're watching for and why it matters downstream.

### 2a. Vision / Mission / Positioning  →  Observe his language

What we capture:
- **Phrases he repeats unprompted** when talking about PCK, clients, or the team. Founders give away their real positioning in the words they recycle.
- **How he describes PCK to outsiders** (a phone call, a meeting, a vendor) vs. how he describes it internally — the gap reveals positioning truth.
- **What makes him animated vs. flat.** Genuine vision shows up in energy, not slogans.
- **Stories he tells.** What he chooses to retell (the Forestias deal, the Beckham launch, a credit fund win) is the de facto mythology of the company.

Tool: keep a verbatim quote log. We will mine it for the Vision/Mission draft.

### 2b. Org Chart + headcount plan  →  Observe the work, not the titles

What we capture:
- **Who actually does what** among the 5 remaining staff (2 legal + 3 credit). Job titles will be misleading.
- **What work he is doing that should not be a CEO's work** — these are the missing roles.
- **What work is falling through the cracks** entirely — these are the blind-spot roles.
- **What the 6 subsidiaries pull from him** — even though we're focused on parent, the parent's headcount has to absorb subsidiary load.
- **The "single points of failure"** — what stops if any one of the 5 takes leave.

Tool: a simple work-log grid (person × hour × activity) for the day.

### 2c. "Ownership Mindset" culture codification  →  Observe what he reinforces

This is the most important capture of the day. Watch:
- **Moments he praises someone** — what behavior triggered it? That is the operational definition of "ownership."
- **Moments he is frustrated** — what behavior triggered it? That is the operational definition of "employee mindset" he wants to eliminate.
- **The MLM/insurance comparison** — ask him at a quiet moment to point to a specific staff member or past hire who *had* the mindset he wants. Real examples beat abstract values.
- **How he talks about rejection / lost deals / difficult clients** — does he model the resilience he wants to install?
- **The Thai word he reaches for.** "เจ้าของ" (owner)? "นักรบ" (warrior)? "ลูกหม้อ" (loyalist)? The vocabulary is the culture.

Tool: a two-column "Reinforces / Frustrates" log. By end of day we should have 15–20 concrete behaviors on each side.

### 2d. Onboarding & training system  →  Observe him training

He currently trains staff personally on product / sales / mindset. We need to capture:
- **The actual content** — what does he teach? Product knowledge? Deal structuring? Client handling?
- **The method** — Socratic? Lecture? Show-and-do? War stories?
- **The sequence** — what comes first, second, third in how he brings someone up to speed?
- **What he assumes the trainee already knows** (these are the implicit prerequisites we have to make explicit in onboarding).
- **Where he gets impatient or repeats himself** — those are the recurring training gaps we need to systematize.

If possible, ask him to walk through training a hypothetical new RM out loud. Record (with consent). This becomes the seed of the onboarding curriculum.

### 2e. Milestone-Based Commission Structure  →  Observe the deal cycle

What we capture:
- **The actual stages a deal goes through** at PCK — from sourcing to document approval to negotiation to legal to board to close. Timestamps matter.
- **Which stages take the longest** (the cash-flow pain points for RMs).
- **Who touches the deal at each stage** (RM, legal, credit, Khun Nut, board).
- **Where deals die** — and at which stage the RM's "ownership" actually pays off.
- **What "value delivered" looks like at each milestone** — so commission triggers map to real progress, not vanity events.

Tool: a deal-cycle flowchart sketched live as he walks us through one or two real deals.

### 2f. SOPs with risk-grading (1-2-3-4)  →  Observe his judgment calls

What we capture:
- **How he risk-grades a loan in real time** — what does he ask, what does he check, what makes him say yes/no/maybe?
- **The intuitive heuristics** he uses that aren't written down anywhere. These are the SOP gold.
- **The questions he asks his credit team** — those are the SOP checklist items.
- **What level of decision he keeps for himself vs. delegates** — the line between 1-2 (junior-decidable) and 3-4 (CEO-decidable) lives here.

Ask directly at a calm moment: "ถ้าจะให้น้องเครดิตตัดสินใจเองได้ในเคสเกรด 1-2 คุณณัฐต้องเห็นอะไรในเอกสารบ้างถึงจะวางใจ?" Capture verbatim.

### 2g. EA JD (for Khun Chon) + RM JD  →  Shadow Khun Chon too, if possible

What we capture:
- **What Khun Chon actually does day-to-day** — the EA JD has to match this, not a generic EA template.
- **Where Khun Chon is bottlenecked** — those are the EA's job.
- **The interaction pattern between Khun Nut and Khun Chon** — informs the EA's authority boundaries.
- **What kind of person could thrive between these two executives** — informs the cultural fit screen.

If we can get even 2 hours with Khun Chon directly, take it. The EA hire is for him and we can't write the JD blind.

### 2h. Decentralized Management + NDA framework  →  Observe decision points

What we capture:
- **Every decision that lands on his desk in the day** — log it, categorize it (could a manager have made this? could an SOP have made this? did it genuinely need CEO?).
- **What information he sees that others don't** — those are the confidentiality boundaries.
- **What he refuses to delegate** — and why. Distinguish "won't" (control issue) from "can't" (no one trained yet) from "shouldn't" (real CEO call).
- **What sensitive material flows through informal channels** (Line, verbal) that should be NDA-protected.

### 2i. Disciplinary framework ("too kind" handling)  →  Observe the moments

This is the most relationally sensitive capture. We are watching for:
- **A mistake or underperformance moment.** What does he do? Soften? Absorb? Avoid? Use "ไม้เรียว" symbolically?
- **The aftermath.** Does the behavior recur? Does the team member learn?
- **His own narration** ("I should have said something but...") — these are the moments to gently surface in debrief.
- **What he *does* do well disciplinarily** — there will be something. Build the framework around that, not against him.

We are not capturing this to write a report saying "the CEO is too kind." We are capturing this to design a disciplinary process that respects his temperament while removing the parts that hurt the business — likely a structured "feedback in private + written record kept internally + escalation path that doesn't require him to wield the cane himself" model. Possibly involving the future MD or HR lead carrying the harder conversations.

### 2j. Founder bandwidth + sustainability  →  Observe his energy curve

His day runs 6–7 AM to 2–4 AM. This is not sustainable and it is not optional for him short-term. What we capture:
- **When his energy and judgment are sharpest** (likely morning) — protect that for strategic work in the redesign.
- **When fatigue shows** (likely late afternoon if he's already been up since 6) — these are the hours to systematize, not lean on him for.
- **Which late-night blocks are genuinely irreplaceable** (international fund calls across time zones) vs. which are habit.
- **What he does in his "off" moments** — gym, commute, meals — these are the windows we may be able to schedule short syncs into without adding to his load.

## 3. The shadowing day(s) playbook

### Pre-day prep (the week before)

- [ ] **Lock the day(s).** Pick days that include at least one client meeting, one internal team interaction, and one deal-review moment. Avoid pure travel days unless the travel itself is the workstream.
- [ ] **Send a one-page brief to Khun Nut** restating: scope, what we'll do, what we won't do, what we'll deliver back, what consent we need for recording.
- [ ] **Get explicit consent** for: voice memos during quiet observation moments, photos of whiteboards/documents (no client-identifying material), and 1:1 follow-up conversations with the 5 staff and Khun Chon.
- [ ] **Brief the 5 staff** through Khun Nut: "พี่ ๆ น้อง ๆ ทีม Sai Fah La Mun จะมาเดินสังเกตการทำงานวันที่ X เป็นการช่วยออกแบบระบบ HR ใหม่ ไม่ใช่การประเมินใคร ขอความร่วมมือทำงานตามปกติเลย" — short, clear, non-threatening.
- [ ] **Decide who shadows what.** Suggested split:
  - **Tanny** stays with Khun Nut throughout (primary observer, relational anchor).
  - **Joy** floats — does parallel observation of the 5 staff and, if available, Khun Chon. Coordinates logistics.
  - Sadao/Puk not needed for this; this is HR workstream.
- [ ] **Prepare capture tools:**
  - Paper notebook + pen (less intrusive than laptop in Thai SME settings)
  - Phone for voice memos (with consent, used sparingly)
  - A printed observation grid for each of the 9 deliverable categories (see Section 2) so we tag observations as we go
  - A simple deal-cycle blank flowchart to fill in live
  - "Reinforces / Frustrates" two-column sheet for the ownership-mindset capture
- [ ] **Pre-write the questions** we want to ask in calm moments. Do NOT improvise — we will forget the most important ones. Suggested ten:
  1. "เล่าให้ฟังหน่อยว่าวันที่คุณณัฐภูมิใจที่สุดกับทีมคือวันไหน ทำไม?"
  2. "ในทีม 5 คนตอนนี้ ใครมี mindset ที่คุณณัฐอยากให้เป็น template?"
  3. "ถ้าได้ MD คนใหม่ที่ทำงานได้จริง — สิ่งแรกที่คุณณัฐอยากปล่อยมือคืออะไร?"
  4. "เรื่องที่ทำให้คุณณัฐนอนไม่หลับช่วงนี้คืออะไร?"
  5. "MD สองคนที่ผ่านมา — ถ้ามองย้อนกลับไป สิ่งที่คุณณัฐน่าจะทำต่างคืออะไร?"
  6. "Ownership mindset ของคุณณัฐ ถ้าให้บอกเป็น 3 พฤติกรรมที่ดูได้ คือ?"
  7. "ตอนน้องเครดิตทำพลาด ความรู้สึกแรกของคุณณัฐคือ?"
  8. "ลูกค้าแบบไหนที่คุณณัฐปฏิเสธ — แม้จะปิดดีลได้?"
  9. "ใน 12 เดือนข้างหน้า ถ้าทีม parent ขึ้นไปที่ 15–20 คน — หน้าตาทีมเป็นยังไง?"
  10. "ถ้าวันนี้ทำงานน้อยลง 30% ได้ คุณณัฐจะใช้เวลาที่ได้คืนทำอะไร?"

### Day-of structure (the operational rhythm)

Adapt to his actual schedule. Suggested observation blocks:

**Block A — Pre-day call (the day before, 30 min)**
Confirm tomorrow's calendar with him. Identify which blocks we'll observe live, which we'll get summarized debrief on, which we'll skip (e.g., personal/family). Re-confirm consent.

**Block B — Morning observation (gym → first meeting, ~7:00–10:00)**
- If he is open to it, join the tail end of his gym/morning routine for the *commute or breakfast* — this is when CEOs talk most openly. Do NOT shadow inside the gym itself; too invasive.
- Be present from arrival at the office. Watch the *first 30 minutes* carefully — how he greets staff, what he checks first, what mood he sets.
- This is the highest-energy block. Prioritize observing his strategic / decision-making behavior here.

**Block C — Internal team interactions (mid-morning)**
- Sit in (silently) on any team huddle or 1:1.
- Watch the 5 staff's body language as much as his words. The team's reactions are data.
- Track who he gives work to, who he checks in on, who he avoids.

**Block D — Client / deal work (late morning to afternoon)**
- Observe a deal review or client call if possible.
- This is where ownership-mindset reinforcement, risk-grading judgment, and deal-cycle stages all surface together.
- Capture verbatim the questions he asks his team about a deal.

**Block E — The "ride-along" (transitions between meetings)**
- Walking from meeting to meeting, in the car between Central World and the office — this is gold for honest conversation.
- Use the pre-written calm-moment questions here. Pace them; don't fire all ten in 20 minutes.

**Block F — Khun Nut training someone (if it can be engineered)**
- If we can ask him to walk us through what he'd say to a new RM on day 1 — even if no new hire is present, just monologue — this is the single highest-leverage capture for the onboarding/training deliverable.
- Frame as: "เพื่อให้เราออกแบบ onboarding ให้ตรงกับคุณณัฐ ขอ 30 นาที — สมมุติว่ามีน้อง RM ใหม่วันแรก คุณณัฐจะสอนอะไรเขาก่อน?"

**Block G — Late afternoon (5–8 PM)**
- Often the most chaotic / draining block for founders. Observation here reveals what fatigue does to his decision quality.
- Quietly note: how many decisions land on him in this window? How many should have been pre-decided by SOP?

**Block H — Evening / international call window (8 PM onwards)**
- Don't shadow live unless he wants us there. Instead, agree to a 15-min debrief *the next morning* on what happened in the international calls — what decisions, what tensions, what wins.
- Respect that this is when his strategic / partner-facing work happens; our presence might shift it.

**Block I — End-of-day micro-debrief (whenever the day actually ends, even briefly)**
- 5–10 minutes. One question: "อะไรที่วันนี้พลังงานหมดไปกับเรื่องที่ไม่น่าจะต้องทำเองครับ/คะ?"
- Capture his answer raw. This is the founder-bandwidth deliverable in a single sentence.

### What NOT to do

- **Don't interrogate.** This is shadowing, not interview. Questions are for transition moments only.
- **Don't take notes visibly in front of the 5 staff.** They will perform or freeze. Step away to capture; or capture later from memory + voice memo.
- **Don't speak to staff *about* Khun Nut on Day 1.** Earn the room first. Save 1:1 staff conversations for Day 2 or post-shadowing.
- **Don't react visibly to "too kind" moments.** No raised eyebrows, no "you should have...". Note it, debrief privately later.
- **Don't reference the failed MDs unprompted.** If he raises them, listen. If not, save for a calm 1:1 in the debrief.
- **Don't try to add value during the day.** Resist the urge to suggest, advise, or fix. Pure observation is the value. Advice is for the post-synthesis.
- **Don't shadow in the gym, the bathroom, family calls, or personal medical/financial calls.** Hard line.
- **Don't bring multiple Sai Fah La Mun people into the same meeting.** One observer per room max. Two of us in a 6-person huddle changes the dynamic.

### Real-time artifacts to capture

For each block, walk away with:
- **Verbatim quote log** (his words, not your paraphrase) — minimum 20 quotes for the day
- **Decision log** (every decision that hits his desk, categorized: SOP-able / manager-able / genuine CEO call)
- **Reinforces / Frustrates log** (15+ entries each by end of day)
- **Work-log grid** (the 5 staff × time blocks × activity)
- **Deal-cycle sketch** (filled in from at least one real deal walkthrough)
- **Energy/fatigue map** (his energy across the day, your read)
- **Photos** (with consent, no client material) — whiteboards, his desk setup, the office layout
- **Three "huh, that's interesting" notes per block** — your gut reactions, things you didn't expect

## 5. Post-shadowing synthesis approach

### Within 24 hours (Tanny solo)
- Transcribe voice memos
- Type up all paper notes
- Tag every observation with its deliverable category (2a–2j)
- Star the 10 most important moments of the day

### Within 48 hours (Tanny + Joy, 90 min)
- Walk through the observation grids together (catches each other's blind spots)
- Cross-check Joy's parallel observations of the 5 staff and Khun Chon against Tanny's observations of Khun Nut
- Draft the **"Operational Definition of Ownership Mindset at PCK"** — a one-pager, in his own words and behaviors, ready to anchor every downstream artifact
- Identify the 3 biggest "huh" insights — the things we didn't expect and that change our design assumptions

### Within 7 days — feed observations into draft deliverables
Direct mapping from observation category → first-draft artifact:
- 2a (language) → Vision/Mission/Positioning draft (in his words)
- 2b (work) → Org chart current-state + 12-month future-state
- 2c (mindset) → Ownership Mindset one-pager + culture statement
- 2d (training) → Onboarding curriculum skeleton (Week 1, Month 1, Quarter 1)
- 2e (deal cycle) → Milestone Commission Structure v1
- 2f (judgment) → SOP risk-grading framework (1-2-3-4 criteria draft)
- 2g (executive load) → EA JD (for Khun Chon) + RM JD
- 2h (decisions) → Delegation matrix + NDA framework outline
- 2i (discipline) → Disciplinary framework proposal designed around his temperament
- 2j (bandwidth) → CEO time-protection recommendations for the Roadmap intro

### Debrief with Khun Nut (within 7 days, private, 60–90 min)
Three sections, in this order:
1. **What we saw that's working** (lead with strength — earn the right to the hard part)
2. **What we saw that surprised us** (neutral curiosity, not judgment)
3. **The two or three patterns we think are limiting the scale** (named directly, with concrete examples from the day — *not* abstract advice)

Close with: "Roadmap presentation [date]. Here's what we'll bring back. Anything you want us to dig deeper on before then?"

### Then the HR Roadmap presentation
By the time we present, every recommendation is traceable to a moment we observed. That's the trust-builder. That's also why the previous two MDs failed and we won't.

## 6. Risks and things to handle carefully

**Risk: He performs a curated day for us.**
Mitigation: Two days, not one. The first day is performed; the second is real. If we only get one day, schedule it on a day he can't control (a deal-crisis day, an end-of-month day) rather than a "nice" day.

**Risk: The 5 staff freeze up or perform for us.**
Mitigation: Joy floats and chats casually; we do not "observe" them in any clipboard sense on Day 1. Save real staff 1:1s for after the shadowing, framed as "context for the Roadmap" not "evaluation."

**Risk: We see "too kind" moments and react visibly.**
Mitigation: Pre-agreed internal signal between Tanny and Joy — a glance, not a comment. Process in the car, not in the room.

**Risk: He asks us in the moment "what should I do?"**
Mitigation: Pre-prepared answer: "ขอจดไว้ก่อนนะคะ เดี๋ยว debrief เราจะตอบให้ครบ — ตอนนี้อยากเห็นวิธีคุณณัฐทำก่อน" Resist the urge to advise mid-day; it contaminates the observation.

**Risk: He shares something deeply personal or confidential (family, mental health, finances).**
Mitigation: Listen. Don't take notes on it. Don't include in any written artifact. Treat as a lawyer or priest would. The relational deposit from holding this well is enormous.

**Risk: The two-failed-MDs topic comes up emotionally.**
Mitigation: Let him talk. Don't problem-solve. Don't compare or analyze the two MDs in front of him. Note for ourselves: the wounds will inform the disciplinary framework design.

**Risk: We confuse "shadowing the CEO" with "auditing the company."**
Mitigation: Our scope is HR foundation for the parent company. We are not assessing the credit book, the subsidiaries' performance, the M&A pipeline, or the property portfolio. Stay in lane. If we see something material outside our scope (e.g., obvious compliance risk), flag privately in debrief, don't act.

**Risk: He gets tired by mid-afternoon and shadowing becomes a burden.**
Mitigation: Build in a soft exit ("we'll step out after the 3pm meeting and reconnect tomorrow morning") so we leave him space to breathe. We can always extend if he wants us to stay.

**Risk: Cultural — being seen as "ฝรั่ง / consultant" who is judging the Thai SME founder.**
Mitigation: Thai politeness register throughout. Pay attention to ครับ/ค่ะ, who you ไหว้ first, whether to remove shoes, how to greet senior vs junior staff. Joy can take the lead on these protocols if helpful. Show up dressed appropriately for *his* office, not for a Bangkok corporate.

**Risk: He uses the day to "vent" rather than work normally.**
Mitigation: This is actually a gift if it happens — capture it. But re-anchor gently: "อยากเห็นคุณณัฐทำงานปกติ ๆ ด้วยนะคะ ระหว่างวัน — เผื่อจะได้เห็นจังหวะจริง ๆ"

## 7. Open questions / decisions before we lock the day
