# Promoting Your Best Doer to Manager

## Idea
**Hook**: Promoting your best 'Doer' to a 'Manager'? You just lost a worker and gained a terrible boss.

**Language**: EN

**Theme**: Warning

**Angle**: Classic mistake — great individual contributors don't automatically become great managers. Different skill sets entirely.

**Talking Points**:
- A great IC is paid for output. A great manager is paid for the team's output. Different sport, different muscles.
- Promote without prep → they micromanage what they used to own, burn out doing both jobs, and the team starts looking for the door.
- Two real fixes: train them 6 months BEFORE the title change, OR build a senior IC track so doers can grow without managing.

```
[HOOK — 5s]
Promoting your best 'Doer' to a 'Manager'? You just lost a worker and gained a terrible boss.

[BODY — 35s]
Here's the trap. Your top performer was great because they did the work — fast, accurate, reliable. But managing isn't doing. It's coaching, delegating, and giving feedback that lands.
Throw them in cold and three things happen. They micromanage what they used to own. They burn out trying to do their old job AND the new one. And the team starts looking for the door.
The fix? Two paths. One — train them six months BEFORE the title change. Two — build a senior IC track so your best doers can grow without managing.

[CTA — 5s]
Don't promote talent into a job they were never set up to win. Follow for more management traps to avoid.
```
