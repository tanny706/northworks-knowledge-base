# A Toxic High-Performer Is a Virus

## Idea
**Hook**: A 'Toxic' High-Performer is a virus. Fire them before they infect your whole team!

**Language**: EN

**Theme**: Warning

**Angle**: Strong take — results don't excuse toxic behavior. The hidden cost to culture and retention is far greater.

**Talking Points**:
- The math: their results are visible — the damage to the team is hidden (quiet quitting, lost referrals, drained morale).
- Your top performer leaves the moment you tolerate the toxic one. Tolerance signals what you actually value.
- The rule — results are necessary, never sufficient. HOW they win matters as much as IF they win.

[HOOK — 5s]
A 'toxic' high-performer is a virus. Fire them before they infect your whole team.

[BODY — 35s]
I know — they hit the numbers. They're hard to replace. So you tolerate the rudeness, the gossip, the 'I'm above the rules.'
Here's what you don't see. Your other performers are quietly checking out. Your top talent is updating their LinkedIn. Trust in your leadership is leaking out the door.
You're not keeping a star. You're paying their salary AND paying for the people they're driving away.

Keeping them costs more than firing them. Every. Single. Time.
