# Confusing Busy with Productive

## Idea
**Hook**: Confusing 'Busy' with 'Productive'? Your payroll is leaking money every single day.

**Language**: EN

**Theme**: Warning

**Angle**: Activity ≠ output. Measuring the wrong things creates the wrong culture and wastes budget.

**Talking Points**:
- Heads-down + packed calendars + Slack pinging = looks productive, often isn't.
- 3 diagnostic questions: who would be missed, what got DECIDED, would I pay for that meeting hourly?
- What gets measured gets done — measure activity, you get activity; measure outcomes, you get outcomes.

---

## Script

> **Status**: `[x] draft` `[ ] reviewed` `[ ] final`
> **Recorded**: `[ ] yes` — Date:

**Length target**: ~45 seconds (~115 words)

```
[HOOK — 5s]
Confusing 'busy' with 'productive'? Your payroll is leaking money every single day.

[BODY — 35s]
Here's the trap. You walk through the office. Everyone's heads-down. Calendars are packed. Slack is pinging. Looks great, right?
But busy isn't the goal. Output is.
Ask yourself three questions.
One — if this person took today off, what would actually be missing tomorrow?
Two — what did they DECIDE this week, not just attend?
Three — would I pay for that meeting if it was billed by the hour?
What gets measured gets done. Measure activity, you get activity. Measure outcomes, you get outcomes.

[CTA — 5s]
Stop rewarding 'looks busy.' Start rewarding 'ships results.' Follow for more leadership wake-up calls.
```
