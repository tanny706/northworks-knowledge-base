## Purpose of This Document

This document is the required first step before any legal or formal actions are taken — including name registration, company transfer, or client onboarding.

It addresses three critical questions raised by our advisor:

1. What is our core business — and how does it differ from our existing businesses?
2. How do we divide income fairly given P Ple's larger network?
3. How do we protect Northworks and P Ple's existing business?

## 1. The Two Businesses — How They Are Different

Before defining Best Workplace's model, it is essential to understand how it relates to Northworks. These are not competing businesses. They operate at different levels, for different clients, in complementary ways.

### 1.1 Side-by-Side Comparison

| | Northworks | Best Workplace |
|---|---|---|
| **Core service** | Fractional / Interim HR Leadership | LMS — scalable learning products |
| **Model** | Tanny embeds inside the organization | Teach and coach people to do it themselves |
| **Clients** | Startup CEOs & scaling companies (B2B) | HR professionals, individuals, SMEs (B2C + B2B) |
| **What is sold** | Tanny's time & expertise directly | Courses, coaching programs, templates |
| **Engagement** | Long-term, retained relationships | Project-based, product-based, scalable |
| **Other services** | AI consulting, CEO advisory, Office Build/Expansion, Event Management | Coaching programs, HR consulting (large project basis), templates |
| **Boundary** | Does NOT do training | Does NOT do fractional/embedded HR leadership |
| **Revenue ceiling** | Scales with Tanny's hours (cap exists) | Scales with products — no ceiling |

### The Key Insight

> **Northworks** = Tanny goes in and does it FOR the client.
> **Best Workplace** = we teach, coach, and equip people to do it THEMSELVES.

These two businesses **feed each other**:

- A Northworks client who finishes a fractional engagement can transition to Best Workplace's LMS for ongoing learning.
- A Best Workplace client who needs deeper HR support can be referred to Northworks.
- The brands serve different maturity stages of the same audience — they do not compete.

## 2. Best Workplace — Core Business Definition

Best Workplace is a scalable HR learning and coaching platform. Unlike Northworks (which is bounded by Tanny's personal hours), Best Workplace is designed from the ground up to grow without a ceiling.

### 2.1 Core Product: LMS (Learning Management System)

**Why LMS is the core**

- The LMS is the scalable engine of Best Workplace.
- Everything else — training, coaching, templates — feeds into it or lives on it.
- A client can access the LMS 24/7 without requiring either partner's time.
- It creates recurring revenue and positions Best Workplace as a *product* company, not just a service company.

| Service | What It Is | Target Client |
|---|---|---|
| **LMS (Core)** | Online courses, training modules, HR templates, and toolkits. Clients access independently. Passive and recurring revenue. | HR professionals, individuals, SMEs wanting self-paced learning |
| **HR Training** | Live or recorded training on labor law, PIP, HR 101, HR AI. Can be standalone or bundled into LMS. | Companies needing structured HR education for their teams |
| **Coaching Programs** | Business, performance, manager, and employee coaching. Structured programs, not ad hoc sessions. Delivered by both partners. | Leaders, managers, and employees seeking guided development |
| **HR Consulting (Projects)** | For large, complex engagements that go beyond the LMS — policy design, org restructuring, HR system builds. Project-based, not retained. | SMEs and larger companies with specific one-time HR needs |

### 2.2 What Best Workplace Does NOT Do

To maintain a clear boundary with Northworks and stay focused:

- Does **NOT** offer fractional or interim HR leadership (embedded, retained) — that is Northworks
- Does **NOT** take on CEO advisory or executive strategy work — that is Northworks
- Does **NOT** manage Office Build/Expansion or Event Management — that is Northworks
- Does **NOT** sell Tanny's personal time as the primary product — products and programs scale independently

## 3. Income Division Framework

P Ple brings a larger network, which creates natural asymmetry in client origination. The model below rewards both *who brings the client* AND *who does the work* — fairly, at every stage of the partnership.

### 3.1 The Challenge

> **The Problem We Need to Solve**
>
> P Ple has more connections than Tanny right now — meaning P Ple is likely to bring in more clients initially.
> A flat 50/50 split would be unfair to P Ple. But a pure commission model creates instability and competition.
> We need a model that: (a) rewards who brings the client, (b) rewards who does the work, and (c) keeps the partnership healthy long-term.

### 3.2 Recommended Model: Base Split + Origination Bonus + Delivery Credit

| Layer | How It Works | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Layer 1 — Base Split** | Revenue from shared work (joint delivery, shared brand) is split 50/50 after deducting operating costs. | Equal ownership of shared business and brand. |
| **Layer 2 — Origination Bonus** | The partner who BROUGHT the client earns an additional 10–15% origination fee on that client's revenue (before the 50/50 split). | Rewards the person who used their network to grow the business. |
| **Layer 3 — Delivery Credit** | If one partner does significantly more delivery work (e.g., 80% of hours), that partner earns a proportional delivery fee, agreed per project. | Rewards actual time and effort — prevents silent overload. |

### 3.3 Illustrative Example

P Ple brings in a client. Total project value: **200,000 THB**.

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Operating costs deducted first (est. 20%) | −40,000 THB |
| Remaining pool | 160,000 THB |
| P Ple origination bonus (12.5% of 200k) | +25,000 THB to P Ple |
| Remaining for 50/50 split | 135,000 THB |
| Tanny receives (50%) | 67,500 THB |
| P Ple receives (50% + origination) | 67,500 + 25,000 = **92,500 THB** |
| If Tanny did 70% of delivery work | Additional delivery fee negotiated per project |

### 3.4 LMS / Passive Revenue — Special Case

Revenue from the LMS (course purchases, template sales) that is not tied to a specific client originator is split 50/50 after operating costs. Both partners contribute to content creation; contributions are tracked and reviewed quarterly to ensure fairness.

## 4. Protecting Both Existing Businesses

Because Northworks and Best Workplace complement each other, protecting them is not about keeping them separate — it is about making the referral and collaboration model **explicit and fair**.

### 4.1 The Three Businesses at a Glance

| Business | Role |
|---|---|
| **Northworks** | Tanny's existing business. Continues operating independently as a Fractional HR Leadership service. Northworks is a FEEDER for Best Workplace — not a competitor. |
| **P Ple's Business** | P Ple's existing business. Continues operating independently. Same principle: P Ple chooses which clients to refer, and her business is compensated. |
| **Best Workplace** | The shared venture. Takes on new clients in HR training, coaching, LMS, and large HR consulting projects. Does NOT poach existing clients without a formal referral agreement. |

### 4.2 Client Flow & Referral Rules

| Scenario | What Happens | Who Leads |
|---|---|---|
| Existing Northworks client needs training or coaching | Northworks refers to Best Workplace. Northworks receives a referral fee (suggest: 10% of first contract value). | Tanny |
| Existing P Ple client needs HR training or LMS | P Ple's business refers to Best Workplace. P Ple's business receives a referral fee. | P Ple |
| Best Workplace client needs fractional HR leadership | Best Workplace refers to Northworks. Best Workplace receives a referral fee from Northworks. | Tanny |
| New client brought by P Ple (her network) | Goes to Best Workplace. P Ple earns origination bonus. | P Ple + Best Workplace |
| New client brought by Tanny | Goes to Best Workplace. Tanny earns origination bonus. | Tanny + Best Workplace |
| New client from shared brand / marketing | Goes to Best Workplace. Shared credit — no origination bonus. | Best Workplace |

### 4.3 Workload Protection

Neither partner reduces their existing business commitments to serve Best Workplace without mutual agreement. Each quarter, both partners review:

- Total hours split between existing businesses vs. Best Workplace
- Whether revenue from Best Workplace justifies the time invested
- Whether either partner's existing business is being negatively impacted

If imbalance is found, partners adjust scope, fees, or responsibilities by mutual agreement.

## 5. Roles & Responsibilities

With the business model clear, both partners need to agree on who does what — including all behind-the-scenes work.

| Area | Who Does It | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| LMS Content Creation | Both partners contribute; review contribution quarterly | Track hours; adjust if significantly unequal |
| HR Training Delivery | Both partners based on expertise and availability | Delivery credit applied if imbalanced per project |
| Coaching Delivery | Both partners based on expertise | Track by project |
| Large HR Consulting Projects | Partner most suited to the engagement; other provides support | Delivery credit model applies |
| Sales & Business Development | Both; P Ple leads initially (larger network) | Origination bonus model handles compensation |
| Marketing & Brand | Both partners; agree responsibilities each quarter | Review quarterly |
| Operations & Admin | To be confirmed — one partner designated as lead | Consider an operations fee if load is heavy |
| Finance & Invoicing | One partner designated as lead (decide together) | Review quarterly |
| Client Relationship Management | Originating partner leads; both stay informed | Standard practice |
